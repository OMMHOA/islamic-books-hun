double "forty feet" (~12 m) — not a trivial rounding difference. Recommend fixing both:
ENG "forty feet" → "forty cubits" (or "forty *dhirā'*"); HUN "negyven láb" → "negyven
könyök" (or "negyven *dhirā'*"), per the fidelity policy (corroborated English-edition

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.246 / HUN ~4115 (ENG ~4144)
AR: "والتقى بخالد بن الوليد. **وكان خالد قد أجمع أمره على الإسلام وانتوى الذهاب** إلى النبى
ﷺ فى مهجره ليتبعه، قال له عمرو: أين يا أبا سليمان؟"
— "He met Khālid ibn al-Walīd. Khālid **had resolved on Islam and intended to go** to the
Prophet in his place of emigration to follow him. 'Amr said to him: 'Where [are you going],
Abū Sulaymān?'"
ENG: "He met Khālid ibn al Walid, who **had decided to accept Islām and migrated to
Madīnah**, and he asked him where he was going."
HUN: "Találkozott Khālid ibn al Waliddal, aki elhatározta, hogy felveszi az iszlámot, és
**kivándorolt Madīnába**, és megkérdezte tőle, hová tart."
→ AR says Khālid had merely *resolved/intended* (انتوى) to go to Madīnah — he had not yet
migrated. ENG's "migrated to Madīnah" (past, completed) contradicts the very next clause,
where 'Amr then asks him "where are you going?" — a question that only makes sense if
Khālid has not yet left. This is a genuine ENG-edition mistranslation (aorist/intention
rendered as completed past action) that creates a logical inconsistency, propagated
verbatim into HUN. Recommend: ENG "migrated to Madīnah" → "intended to migrate to Madīnah"
(or "was resolved to go to... to follow him"); HUN "kivándorolt Madīnába" → "el akart
vándorolni Madīnába" / "úgy döntött, hogy Madīnába megy" (i.e. future/intent, not completed
emigration) — per fidelity policy, fix both, no translator's note needed (plain
mistranslation, not an Arabic-original issue).
