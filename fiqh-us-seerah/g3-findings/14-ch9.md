# G3 slice 14 — Chapter 9 ("A Legfőbb Társ") + Epilogue ("Utószó")

SLICE: HUN lines 6151–6312 (ch9 6151–6297, Utószó 6299–6311) vs AR printed pp.354–364 (AR-full lines 8284–8623).

Method: HUN verified paragraph-by-paragraph and footnote-by-footnote against AR-full (source of truth), with ENG-full consulted to classify each divergence. Albānī signature date re-checked on the rendered Arabic scan (PDF p.359 = printed p.360).

---

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.356 (fn 1) / HUN ~6265 (fn³)
AR: «ضعيف جدا؛ أخرجه العقيلى فى "الضعفاء"…» — grade = **ضعيف جدا (very weak)**.
ENG: "³ Weak: transmitted by Al-'Uqailī…" — dropped "very".
HUN: "³ Gyenge: Al-'Uqailī hagyományozta a gyenge hadíszok gyűjteményében…" — dropped "very".
→ The Arabic grade is *ḍaʿīf jiddan* = **very weak**; both ENG and HUN flattened it to plain "weak/gyenge". Fix HUN → **"Nagyon gyenge"** (or "Rendkívül gyenge"); fix ENG → "Very weak". (Rest of the footnote — Dhahabī / Ibn Kathīr quotes — matches.)

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.362 / HUN ~6305
AR: «والمسلمون سرت إليهم لوثات **الاحتراف** والتعلق بالقشور والمراسم» — *lawthāt al-iḥtirāf* = the taints of **turning [religion] into a profession/trade / careerism**.
ENG: "The Muslims have been stained with the pollution of **deviation** and adherence to shells and rituals."
HUN: "A muszlimokat beszennyezte **a tévelygés** és a héjakhoz meg szertartásokhoz való ragaszkodás szennye."
→ *al-iḥtirāf* means professionalism / making religion a livelihood, NOT "deviation/tévelygés". ENG mistranslated and HUN inherited it. Fix HUN to convey "a [vallás] hivatássá tételének / a megélhetési célú vallásosságnak a szennye"; fix ENG to "professionalism/careerism".

## [FIX-BOTH] (minor) AR p.362 / HUN ~6303
AR: «…حتى تضمن حياة، لدعائمها الأولى من تثاليث وقرابين» — "…to guarantee **a life** for its primary tenets…". No exclamation.
ENG: "…to guarantee life **(and what a life!)** for its primary tenets…"
HUN: "…hogy életet **(és micsoda életet!)** biztosítson alaptételeinek…"
→ The parenthetical "(and what a life!)"/"(és micsoda életet!)" is an ENG-edition interpolation with no counterpart in the Arabic. Under Arabic-only fidelity, recommend removing it from both files. (Low priority — stylistic, not factual.)

## [AR-AUTHOR-ERROR / HUN OK] AR p.360 (fn) / HUN ~6295 (fn¹⁸)
AR (print + AR-full): «دمشق : ٢٨/ ٥/ **١٩٧٥هـ**» — the Arabic original itself prints "1975" with the hijri marker هـ (verified on the scan, PDF p.359). This is an obvious in-original typo: "1975 هـ" is internally contradictory (1975 is a CE year; 28/5/**1375** AH ≈ Jan 1956, when Albānī did this annotation).
ENG: "…Damascus, 28/5/**1375 AH**." — silently corrected.
HUN: "…Damaszkusz, **1375. 5. 28.** (a hidzsra szerint)." — silently corrected.
→ HUN is **correct** (the intended date is 1375 AH); no HUN fix. This is a print typo in the Arabic original that both ENG and HUN sensibly fixed (like the "United Sates"/"Egyt" class). Note for the record: AR-full transcription faithfully carries the print's "١٩٧٥هـ"; an inline translator's-note is optional and probably unnecessary for a bare date typo.

## [NOTE] AR p.364 / HUN ~6309
AR: «ما بقيت أصوله **العلمية والنفسية** سليمة» — "its **scholarly/scientific and psychological (nafsī)** principles".
ENG: "Islām's **moral and scientific** principles".
HUN: "az iszlám **erkölcsi és tudományos** elvei".
→ *al-nafsiyya* = psychological/spiritual (lelki), not "moral/erkölcsi". ENG imprecision inherited by HUN. Minor; if tightening, HUN → "tudományos és lelki elvei". Flagging, not asserting a required fix.

## [NOTE] AR pp.362–363 / HUN ~6301, 6303
AR uses **الصليبية** ("Crusaderism", distinct from النصرانية "the Christians/Nazarenes", which the author uses elsewhere, e.g. p.359 «اليهود والنصارى» → "zsidók és keresztények").
ENG renders الصليبية as "Christianity"; HUN follows with "kereszténység" (6301 and 6303).
→ Established ENG choice; loses the author's Crusaderism/Christianity nuance but is defensible. No action, just logged.

## [NOTE] AR p.363 / HUN ~6303
AR: «والنفاذ من خلل **الصفوف المتناحرة** بأكبر غُنم لإسرائيل» — "…through the gaps of the **warring ranks**" (unspecified).
ENG/HUN add specificity: "the split within the ranks **of the Muslims**" / "a **muszlimok** sorain belüli szakadás".
→ ENG interpretive addition inherited by HUN. Minor; contextually plausible. Logged only.

## [NOTE] AR p.357 / HUN ~6203
AR: «إنك بعد ثلاث **عبد العصا**» — idiom "in three days you'll be a slave of the staff" (= subordinate / ruled by others).
ENG: "In three days time you'll be **in a critical position**."
HUN: "Három nap múlva **válságos helyzetben** leszel."
→ ENG rendered the idiom loosely (loses the "subordinate/ruled" sense); HUN follows ENG. Idiom is hard to carry; ENG-sourced, so not HUN drift. Logged.

## [NOTE] AR p.354 / HUN ~6159
AR: «وكانت **ظهيرة**…» — *ẓahīra* = **midday/noon**.
ENG: "It was an **afternoon** clouded with gloom." HUN: "**Borongós, komor délután** volt."
→ noon vs afternoon; ENG imprecision inherited by HUN. Trivial. Also AR's «تغمره الرقة» (suffused with tenderness) is dropped in both — minor.

## [NOTE] AR p.364 / HUN ~6311 (end of Epilogue)
AR closes with the colophon «**تم بحمد الله ومنّته**» ("Completed by the grace and favour of Allah"), AR-full line 8621.
ENG and HUN both omit it (HUN ends the Utószó at 6311, then `---`).
→ Dropped closing formula in both. Minor; could be restored for completeness (FIX-BOTH-lite) but purely a colophon.

## [NOTE] AR p.362 / HUN ~6301
AR lists four items: «اتسعت ميادينها، **وتتابعت أمدادها**، وفدحت مغارمها، وكثرت ضحاياها».
ENG/HUN give three: "battle fields wider, costs higher, losses greater" / "csataterek szélesebbek, költségek magasabbak, veszteségek nagyobbak".
→ «تتابعت أمدادها» ("reinforcements/supplies came one after another") dropped in ENG, inherited by HUN. Minor.

## D-item resolutions
- **D-item 1 — HUN ~6196 term (*mā malakat aymānukum*)**: the phrase surfaces at **HUN 6235**: "…meg az, **akit jobb kezünk birtokol**." AR 8456 «الصلاة **وما ملكت أيمانكم**»; ENG "…prayers and **what one's right hand possessed**." HUN's literal rendering **matches AR faithfully** (the slaves/dependants formula). It is idiomatically obscure but not a fidelity error — an optional clarifying gloss (e.g. "amivel jobbotok rendelkezik" / "[a rabszolgákról és alárendeltekről]") could be added, but no fix is required. **Matches AR.**
- **D-item 2 — «الرفيق الأعلى» (al-rafīq al-aʿlā)**: occurs twice — chapter title (HUN 6153 = AR 8284 «الرفيق الأعلى») and the death scene (HUN 6245 = AR 8491 «بل الرفيق الأعلى من الجنة»). HUN renders **"a Legfőbb Társ"** in the title and **"a Legfőbb Társat a paradicsomból"** in the death scene — **consistent and faithful** ("the Highest/Supreme Companion"). No issue.

## Summary
Chapter 9 and the Epilogue are, on the whole, a faithful and accurate rendering of the Arabic. The death narrative, the pre-death sermon, the succession exchange (ʿAbbās/ʿAlī), the prayer-leadership episodes, the tawḥīd warnings, the death scene with *al-rafīq al-aʿlā*, and Abū Bakr's speech + Qur'ān 3:144 all check out clean, and footnotes 1–18 map 1:1 to AR with correct hiteles/gyenge grading — the single exception being fn3's dropped "very".

Two real corrections (both ENG-edition errors propagated into HUN):
1. fn3 grade "very weak" flattened to "weak/Gyenge" (FIX-BOTH).
2. *al-iḥtirāf* mistranslated as "deviation / tévelygés" instead of "professionalism/careerism" (FIX-BOTH).
Plus one minor stylistic interpolation "(és micsoda életet!)" not in AR (FIX-BOTH-lite), and the Albānī date — an in-original Arabic print typo ("1975هـ") that HUN correctly renders as 1375 AH (no HUN fix).
