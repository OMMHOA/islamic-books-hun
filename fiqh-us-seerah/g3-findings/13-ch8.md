## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.344 / HUN ~line 6117 — footnote 9 (marker ⁹): meaning inverted in translation
AR: "وليس هو تمام الحديث الذى قبله **كما قد يتبادر من عبارة المؤلف**" — sense: "...and it is NOT the completion of the preceding ḥadīth, **contrary to what might [wrongly] appear from the author's [al-Ghazālī's] wording**" (i.e., al-Albānī is warning that al-Ghazālī's phrasing in the main text could mislead readers into thinking the two ḥadīths are one continuous report, but they are not).
ENG fn9: "It is not the completion of the above Ḥadīth **as the author's words indicate**."
HUN fn9 (line 6117): "Nem a fenti hadísz folytatása, **ahogyan a szerző szavai sugallják**: a kettő független hadísz..."
→ Both ENG and HUN read as if "the author's words indicate/suggest [correctly] that it is not the completion" — which reverses al-Albānī's actual point, that the author's wording *misleadingly suggests it IS a continuation*, and he is correcting that impression. Recommend rephrasing HUN to something like: „Ez nem a fenti hadísz folytatása, jóllehet a szerző szavai ezt sugallhatják: a kettő független hadísz…" (and analogously fix ENG-full: "It is not the completion of the above Ḥadīth, though the author's words might suggest otherwise."). Subtle but real semantic inversion, inherited from the English edition.

## [AR-AUTHOR-ERROR] AR p.347 / HUN line 6015 — Ibrāhīm's mother's name printed as "مريم" (Maryam) instead of "مارية" (Māriyah)
AR (verified against the rendered PDF page image, printed page 347 = PDF page 346, not just the OCR transcript): "ودخل رسول الله ﷺ **بمريم** التى بعث بها المقوقس إليه بعد أن أسلمت، وحملت منه، ثم وضعت له ابنا أسماه إبراهيم..." — the printed Arabic book itself clearly reads **مريم** ("Maryam"), confirmed by direct page-image inspection (not an OCR artifact of the AR-full transcript).
ENG: "He did have a son from **Maria**, who was sent to him as a gift by the Patriarch of Alexandria..."
HUN (line 6015): "Fia született azonban **Māriyától**, akit Alexandria pátriárkája küldött neki ajándékul..."
→ The historically correct name for the Prophet's Coptic concubine/wife (mother of his son Ibrāhīm) is **Māriyah al-Qibṭīyah** ("مارية"), not "Maryam" — "Maryam" is a different, very common name (that of Jesus's mother) and its use here for Ibrāhīm's mother appears to be a misprint/slip in this Arabic edition. Both ENG-full and HUN already correctly use "Maria"/"Māriyah" rather than literally transliterating the Arabic's "مريم" as "Maryam" — i.e., ENG/HUN already silently corrected this rather than following the fidelity policy's "keep the Arabic + translator's note" rule for author/print errors. Recommend adding an inline translator's note per policy: ENG `[the Arabic prints "Maryam"; the correct name is Māriyah al-Qibṭīyah — translator's note]`, HUN `[az arab eredeti "Mirjam"-ot ír; a helyes név Māriyah al-Qibṭīyah — a ford.]` — or, if the editorial team judges silent correction preferable for such a well-known factual name (as was apparently already decided when the ENG edition was made), at minimum log the AR misprint here for the record. Leaving current HUN wording (Māriyától) as-is is defensible either way; flagging per policy for a decision.

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.348 / HUN ~line 6023 — "Settling Down" opening paragraph: garbled/altered imagery, dropped adhān reference
AR: "...وصحت العقول العليلة فلم تعد تخشى وترجو إلا الله، بعدما ظلت دهورا تعبد أصناما جامدة، **وسمع الأذان للصلوات يشق أجواز الفضاء** خلال الصحراء التى أحياها الإيمان الجديد."
Literal sense: "...and the **ailing minds became sound/healthy** again, no longer fearing and hoping in anyone but Allāh, after ages of worshipping lifeless idols, and **the call to prayer (adhān) was heard cutting through the vastness of the sky/space** across the desert which the new faith had revived."
ENG: "The minds of the conscientious were awakened to fear, and hope in none but Allāh (ﷻ) was heard penetrating the air of the desert which had been revived by the new faith."
HUN (line 6023): "A lelkiismeretesek elméje istenfélelemre ébredt, és az új hittől életre kelt sivatag levegőjét átjárta az egyedül Allahba (ﷻ) vetett remény hangja."
→ Two distinct problems inherited from the English edition into HUN: (1) AR's "sick/ailing minds became sound" (a healing metaphor, العقول العليلة = the ailing minds) is replaced by "the minds of the conscientious were awakened to fear" — a different image entirely (drops the sickness→health metaphor, invents "conscientious" and reassigns "fear" as the object rather than "no longer fearing anyone but Allāh"). (2) AR's specific, vivid detail that **the call to prayer (adhān) was heard** ringing across the desert is replaced with the vaguer "hope... was heard penetrating the air" — the adhān reference is dropped entirely. Recommend restoring both images in ENG-full and HUN: something like (HUN) „...és a beteg elmék meggyógyultak, úgyhogy többé nem féltek és nem reménykedtek senki másban, csak Allahban (ﷻ), miután korszakokon át élettelen bálványokat imádtak; és felhangzott az ima hívó szava (azán), amely átjárta az új hittől életre kelt sivatag egét." Medium-confidence textual finding, but the adhān omission is clear-cut.
