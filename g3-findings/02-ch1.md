## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.45 / HUN ~line 476 (ENG ~line 475)
AR: «وكأنى **كرة** تتدحرج تحت أقدام عملاق» — «كرة» = **a ball** (rolling under a giant's
feet), a simile for the author's feeling of smallness/humility approaching the Prophet's
grave.
ENG: "…feeling very humble as if I was a **bull** being rolled under the feet of some
giant." (ENG ~line 475)
HUN: "…nagyon alázatosnak érezve magam — mintha **bika** volnék, amelyet valami óriás lába

correcting **bika → labda** in HUN and **bull → ball** in ENG-full; candidate addition to
`ENGLISH-EDITION-ERRATA.md` §3 (Mistranslations).

## Summary

Chunk covered: AR pp. 15–45 (whole of Chapter 1, «١ ـ رسالة وإمام» / "Üzenet és egy
vezető" / "A Message and a Leader"), cross-checked against HUN lines 133–663 and ENG
"Chapter One" in full, section by section, including all 19 chapter-1 footnotes.

Findings: **5 FIX-BOTH** (a fully missing footnote — ḍaʿīf grading + Al-Ghazālī's
editorial rebuttal on the "Love Allāh for His bounties" Ḥadīth, entirely absent from both
ENG and HUN; a missing Qur'ān 41:3 reference; Qur'ān 5:117 mislabeled as 5:120; the
corrupted place name 'Afīq→'Aqīq; and the "bull"→"ball" mistranslation of «كرة»), **0
FIX-HUN** (no HUN-only drift found — every divergence from AR traced back to the English
edition and was already present in ENG-full), **0 AR-AUTHOR-ERROR**, **2 NOTE** (the
expanded Qur'ān 1:5–7 quote beyond what AR cites verbatim, harmless; and the record of
the (1)/(2)/(3) numbered-paragraph markers, an inherited ENG/HUN typographic device with
no AR counterpart, judged harmless), and multiple **OK** section-clearances covering the
chapter end to end. No HUN-specific terminology violations (hiteles/jó/gyenge grading was
correct throughout; no honorifics were found misapplied to enemies of Islam; "Mekka"
spelling not applicable in this chapter's text). Chapter 1 is otherwise a faithful,
well-aligned translation; all five FIX-BOTH items are small, well-localized ENG-edition
errors (a Qur'ān-ref digit, a missing partial ref, one corrupted diacritic-adjacent letter
in a place name, and one word-level mistranslation) plus one genuinely substantial gap (the
missing weak-ḥadīth footnote) that editorially matters because it hides a real scholarly
disagreement between Al-Ghazālī and Al-Albānī present in the Arabic original.
