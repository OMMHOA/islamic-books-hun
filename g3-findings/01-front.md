## [NOTE] AR p.5 / HUN line 13
AR: «وأبادر إلى القول بأنى لم أكتب عن صاحب الرسالة العظمى محمد بن عبدالله صلوات الله وسلامه عليه، وفى نفسى هذا المعنى المحدود» — literally "I hasten to say I did not write about **the bearer of the greatest Message**, Muhammad son of 'Abdullāh … with this limited meaning [in mind]" (i.e., unlike the "great personalities" of the opening sentence, Muhammad ﷺ is fundamentally different: he is the bearer of the Message, not merely a "great personality").
ENG (line 16): "I did not write about **the greatest of all the personalities**, Muhammad (ﷺ) … with this limited concept in mind."
HUN (line 13): "nem ezzel a szűkös felfogással írtam minden személyiség **legnagyobbikáról**, Muhammadról (ﷺ)…"
→ Both ENG and HUN reframe "bearer of the greatest Message" (رسالة) as "greatest of all personalities," losing the contrast al-Ghazali is drawing (Muhammad ﷺ isn't just the top of the "great personalities" category from the previous sentence — he's categorically different, the one who brought the Message). Not a factual error and doesn't mislead, just a softened nuance inherited from ENG. No action required; flagging for awareness only.

## [NOTE] AR p.12 / HUN line 67
AR: «قد يرى الأستاذ المحدِّث أن **تحسين** الترمذى **وتصحيح** الحاكم لا تعويل عليهما» — specifically distinguishes that al-Tirmidhī graded the ḥadīth **ḥasan** (تحسين) while al-Ḥākim graded it **ṣaḥīḥ** (تصحيح) — two different grades from two different scholars.
ENG (line 64): "He may reject **the authentication** of this Ḥadīth by Al-Tirmidhī and Al-Hākim" — flattens both to one generic "authentication."
HUN (line 67): "Ő elvetheti e hadísz Al-Tirmidhī és Al-Hākim általi **hitelesítését**" — same flattening, inherited from ENG.
→ Minor terminology loss: in a book explicitly about ḥadīth grading (and whose HUN convention carefully distinguishes hiteles/jó/gyenge), collapsing "Tirmidhī said ḥasan, al-Ḥākim said ṣaḥīḥ" into one generic "hitelesítés" blurs exactly the kind of distinction the book cares about. Low impact (doesn't change the overall argument), so NOTE rather than FIX-BOTH — but worth a look if a future terminology pass revisits this section. Possible fix: "Al-Tirmidhī jónak (ḥasan), Al-Hākim hitelesnek (ṣaḥīḥ) minősítette e hadíszt."

## [NOTE] AR p.9 / HUN line 37
AR: «لقد رأيتهم يزورون **الروضة** مشوقين متلهفين» — "ar-Rawḍah" (the specific garden-area between the Prophet's tomb and minbar in his Mosque), not literally "grave."
ENG (line 40): "I saw them visiting **his grave**…"
HUN (line 37): "Láttam őket, amint … látogatják **sírját**…"
→ Loose but conventional rendering (visiting the Rawḍah is closely associated with visiting the tomb); not misleading to a general reader. NOTE only, no fix needed.

## [NOTE] AR p.13 / HUN line 85
AR: «كتبت إلى نافع **رحمه الله**» — the formula رحمه الله ("may Allah have mercy on him") is conventionally used for the deceased.
ENG (line 82): "I wrote to Nāfi', May Allāh (ﷻ) **bless** him"
HUN (line 85): "Írtam Nāfi'nak — **áldja meg** Allah (ﷻ) —"
→ "Bless" (áldja meg) instead of "have mercy on" (legyen irgalmas hozzá / könyörüljön rajta) is a minor mistranslation of a standard formula, inherited from ENG into HUN identically. Cosmetic; not misleading. NOTE only.
