## [AR-AUTHOR-ERROR] AR p.58 / HUN ~line 895
AR (rendered page re-checked at 250dpi, `p-057.png`, PDF page 57 = printed p.58 — matches
the AR-full.md transcription exactly, so not an OCR artifact): Ibn al-Athīr's Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl
clan list reads «... فتحالفوا فى دار عبدالله بن جُدْعَان ... وكانوا بنى هاشم، وبنى
عبدالمطلب، وبنى أسد بن عبدالعُزَّى، وزهرة بن كلاب، وتيم بن مرة» — i.e. **"Banū Hāshim and
Banū 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib"** (with "'Abd"). A few lines later the same page names an individual,
«الزبير بن عبدالمطلب» (Al-Zubayr ibn 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib), correctly — that occurrence is a
person's name, not the clan.
ENG (~line 894): "They were: the **Banū Hāshim, Banū Muṭṭalib**, Banū Asad ibn 'Abdul
'Uzza, Zuhrah ibn Kilāb and Taym ibn Murrah." (no "'Abd")
HUN (~line 895): "Ezek voltak: a **Banū Hāshim, a Banū Muttalib**, a Banū Asad ibn 'Abdul
'Uzza, Zuhrah ibn Kilāb és Taym ibn Murrah." — matches ENG, i.e. without "'Abd".
→ Historically the two allied clans of the Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl (per Ibn Hishām's Sīrah, the
ultimate source of this Ibn al-Athīr passage) are **Banū Hāshim** and **Banū al-Muṭṭalib**
(descendants of al-Muṭṭalib ibn 'Abd Manāf, Hāshim's brother) — a distinct sibling clan.
"Banū 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib" as a *separate* clan from Banū Hāshim is genealogically incoherent:
'Abd al-Muṭṭalib was Hāshim's own son and by the Prophet's era "Banū Hāshim" essentially
**is** the descendants of 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib — the two names can't denote two different
allied clans in the same list. This confirms the "Banū 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib" reading in the
printed Arabic text is an error carried in this edition (whether inherited from a faulty
manuscript/print of Ibn al-Athīr or introduced locally), not a transcription/OCR slip —
verified on the rendered scan. ENG/HUN's "Banū Muṭṭalib" (no 'Abd) is actually the
historically correct reading and happens to read *better* than the Arabic here, but per
the fidelity policy the Arabic is the sole source of truth and the correction should still
be recorded rather than silently kept. Recommend: per the AR-author-error convention, this
is a borderline case — the error is arguably in the *print/transmission* of Ibn al-Athīr's
quoted text rather than al-Ghazālī's own composition. Given ENG/HUN already carry the
*historically correct* clan name, and the fidelity policy's purpose is to avoid
propagating errors, **no change recommended to ENG/HUN** here (they are already right);
if strict word-for-word fidelity to this AR edition is wanted instead, add an inline note
at this spot — HUN `[az arab eredetiben „Banū 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib" áll — a ford.]` — flagging
the print's non-standard clan name. Leaving as a flagged NOTE-level item for the main
session to decide rather than a hard FIX, since HUN/ENG's current text is not wrong per se.

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.55 fn / HUN ~line 1095 (footnote 13)
AR (footnote ٢, AR p.55): «حديث ضعيف جدا، **علّقه** ابن عبدالبر فى "جامع العلم" (١/ ١١١)
**ووصله** ابن ماجه فى سننه (١/ ٩٨)...» — "a very weak Ḥadīth; Ibn 'Abd al-Barr transmitted
it **mu'allaq** [without a full chain / suspended] in Jāmi' Bayān al-'Ilm, **and Ibn Mājah
connected it** [gave it a full chain, "waṣala"] in his Sunan..." — i.e. Ibn 'Abd al-Barr
left the chain incomplete, but Ibn Mājah is cited as the one who *did* supply a full chain
(the two clauses are opposites, not parallel).
ENG (~line 1130, fn 13): "Ibn 'Abdul Barr left it undecided in his Jāmi' bayān al-'ilm, and
**also Ibn Mājah**." — i.e. ENG makes Ibn Mājah do the *same* thing as Ibn 'Abd al-Barr
("also"), inverting the Arabic's "waṣalahu" (he connected/gave it a full isnād).
HUN (~line 1095, footnote 13): "Ibn 'Abdul Barr eldöntetlenül hagyta a Jāmi' Bayān
al-'Ilmban, Ibn Mājah **szintén**." — same error, copied from ENG (HUN=ENG≠AR).
→ Mistranslation of «وصله» ("connected/supplied a full chain for it", the technical
opposite of «علّقه», "left it suspended/mu'allaq") as "also left it undecided". Recommend
**FIX-BOTH**: ENG "...left it undecided in his Jāmi' bayān al-'ilm; **Ibn Mājah, however,
gave it a connected chain** (waṣalahu) in his Sunan." HUN: "...eldöntetlenül hagyta a
Jāmi' Bayān al-'Ilmban; **Ibn Mājah azonban teljes lánccal közölte** (waṣala) Szunanjában."
This does not change the overall "very weak" grade (gyenge, matches AR "ضعيف جداً" —
correctly rendered), only the description of who did what with the isnād.
