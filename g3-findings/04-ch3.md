## [FIX-HUN] AR pp.71–73 / HUN lines 1130–1142 — footnote-marker scrambling (the predecessor's undocumented discovery, re-found)
AR: page 71 has one footnote (١) after the Mudaththir quote (Bukhārī/Muslim citation). Page 72 restarts its own footnote count 1–6 for six successive facts, in this body order: F2 buzzing-bees report from 'Umar (فعن عمر: "...كدوى النحل" (١)) — **explicitly graded ضعيف "weak" in the footnote text itself** (يونس بن سليم مجهول...); F3 forehead sweating on a cold day (٢, "روى معناه البخارى ... عائشة"); F4 mount kneeling to the ground (٣, "أخرج معناه أحمد والحاكم ... عائشة", **صحيح الإسناد**); F5 Zayd ibn Thābit's thigh nearly breaking (٤, "أخرجه البخارى ... زيد بن ثابت"); F6 the "Holy Spirit inspired my heart" ḥadīth (٥, **حديث صحيح** from three corroborating chains); F7 explanatory note on body/unseen-world contact (٦).
ENG: assigns these the same seven facts **sequentially and correctly** as fn¹ (Mudaththir) … fn²(F2, weak/Tirmidhī) fn³(F3) fn⁴(F4) fn⁵(F5) fn⁶(F6, sound) fn⁷(F7) — verified marker-by-marker against the footnote list at ENG ~1811–1823; every marker's list content matches the fact it's attached to.
HUN: footnote **list** (HUN ~1775–1787) was silently **reordered**: ¹=Mudaththir, ²=F3(sweat/Bukhārī-'Ā'ishah), ³=F4(mount/Aḥmad-Ḥākim), ⁴=F5(Zayd-thigh/Bukhārī), ⁵=**F2 the weak Tirmidhī buzzing-bees ḥadīth**, ⁶=F6(sound Holy-Spirit ḥadīth), ⁷=F7(unseen-world note). But the **body markers were not correspondingly renumbered** (HUN ~1136, 1140, 1142):
- F2 (buzzing bees, 'Umar) — **no marker at all** after "...hallatszott az arca közelében." The weak-ḥadīth grading (list item ⁵) is now floating, unattached to its subject.
- F3 (sweat) — marked ² ✓ (content matches).
- F4 (mount kneeling) — **no marker at all** after "...ha éppen lovagolt rajta."
- Marker ³ is instead misattached to "...Zayd ibn Thābit (رضي الله عنه) combján nyugodott," — wrong spot: list item ³'s content is the mount-kneeling report, not the thigh-resting one.
- Marker ⁴ lands on "...Zayd (رضي الله عنه) combja majdnem eltört." — content of list ⁴ (Bukhārī/Zayd) does match this clause, but AR has only **one** footnote for the whole Zayd-thigh sentence (at its end), so splitting it across ³ and ⁴ is itself an artifact.
- Marker ⁵ appears at the end of the **Holy-Spirit ḥadīth** quote ("...kimerültséget okozzon?"⁵) — but list item ⁵ is the **weak** buzzing-bees ḥadīth. A reader is told the *sound* Holy-Spirit ḥadīth is weak.
- Marker ⁶ appears on "...kezdett kinyilatkoztatni⁶" (the F7 explanatory-note position) — but list item ⁶ is the **sound** Holy-Spirit ḥadīth (F6's actual content), not F7's note.
- List item ⁷ (the genuine unseen-world explanatory note) is **never referenced anywhere in the body** — orphaned.
→ **Worst effect: the ṣaḥīḥ/ḍaʿīf grading is scrambled** — readers following HUN's marker ⁵ land on the weak-ḥadīth text at the *sound* Holy-Spirit report, and the actually-weak buzzing-bees report carries no footnote at all. Recommend: renumber the HUN footnote **list** back to AR/ENG order (² = weak Tirmidhī/buzzing-bees, ³ = sweat/Bukhārī-'Ā'ishah, ⁴ = mount/Aḥmad-Ḥākim, ⁵ = Zayd-thigh/Bukhārī, ⁶ = sound Holy-Spirit ḥadīth, ⁷ = unseen-world note), and reposition body markers: add ² after the buzzing-bees sentence, add ³ after "...ha éppen lovagolt rajta" (mount kneeling), move the thigh-related marker to a single ⁴ at "...combja majdnem eltört", ⁵ at the end of the Holy-Spirit quote, ⁶ at "...kezdett kinyilatkoztatni". Confirmed this is isolated to fn 2–7 only — fn 8 onward (Waraqah, ⁸) realign correctly with content and with AR/ENG from that point through the end of the chapter (spot-checked ⁹, ¹⁰). Not to be confused with the separately-documented, deliberate ¹⁴/¹⁵-skip later in the chapter (CLAUDE.md quirk note).

## [FIX-BOTH] AR p.81 / HUN ~1298
AR: وأغلظت امرأته "سمية" القول لأبى جهل فطعنها بحربة فى **قُبلها** فماتت
ENG: "...his wife, Sumayyah, shouted curses at Abū Jahl; he stuck a spear into her **heart** and she died."
HUN: „...felesége, Sumayyah átkokat kiáltott Abū Jahlra; ő egy lándzsát döfött a **szívébe**, és ő meghalt."
→ AR specifically says Abū Jahl speared her in the **pudendum/genitals** (قُبل), a well-attested and specifically noted detail of Sumayyah bint Khabbāṭ's martyrdom (the deliberate cruelty/humiliation is the point historically). The English edition softened this to "heart," and HUN followed. Recommend correcting both to reflect the Arabic (e.g. HUN: „...ő egy lándzsát döfött a szemérmébe" or similarly accurate phrasing), per the fidelity policy — this is a genuine mistranslation, not an Arabic-author error.
